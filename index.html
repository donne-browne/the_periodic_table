Hydrogen

Hydrogen (H)is a colourless, odourless, tasteless, flammable gaseous substance that is the simplest member of the family of chemical elements. The hydrogen atom has a nucleus made of a proton having one unit of positive electrical charge; an electron, having one unit of negative electrical charge, is also related with this nucleus. Under normal conditions, hydrogen gas is a loose concentration of hydrogen molecules, each made of 2 atoms, or H2.

Helium

Helium (He)is a chemical element & inert gas of Group 18 (noble gases) of the periodic table. The second lightest element, losing to hydrogen. Helium is a colourless, odourless, and tasteless gas that condensates into liquid at −268.9 °C or −452 °F.

Lithium

Lithium (Li) is a chemical element of Group 1 of the periodic table which is the alkali metal group and lightest of the solid elements. The metal itself—which is soft, white, and shiny—and several of its alloys and compounds are made on an industrial level.

Beryllium

Beryllium (Be) formerly glucinium until 1957, is a chemical element & the lightest member of the alkaline-earth metals on the periodic table. It is used in metallurgy as a hardening agent and in many outer space and nuclear items.

Nitrogen

Nitrogen (N)is a nonmetallic element of Group 15 on the periodic table. It is a colourless, odourless, & tasteless gas that is the most plentiful element in Earth’s atmosphere (78% of dry air) and is a part of all living matter.
